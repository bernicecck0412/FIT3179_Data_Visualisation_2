Economic Growth: GDP Per Capita of Malaysia (2015-2024)

This dashboard visualise how Malaysia’s economic performance has evolved over the past decade, highlighting both its global context and internal disparities. It combines global and regional visual perspectives to reveal growth patterns, state rankings, and relative progress across Malaysia’s 14 states and 3 federal territories.

GDP per Capita by State in Malaysia

The map shows the GDP per capita by state in Malaysia from 2015 to 2024. In 2024, federal territories such as Kuala Lumpur recorded the highest GDP per capita, shown by the darker shade, followed by Labuan and Sarawak. States in Peninsular Malaysia such as Selangor and Penang also have relatively high GDP per capita compared to states like Kelantan and Perlis, which remain lighter in color. This indicates that economic activities are more concentrated in urban and industrialized areas. Overall, Malaysia shows regional disparities in GDP per capita distribution.

Malaysia’s GDP by State

Selangor has consistently recorded the highest GDP among all states in Malaysia from 2015 to 2024. There was a noticeable drop in 2020, due to the start of the COVID-19 pandemic, which affected most states' economic performance. However, the GDP gradually recovered in the following years. By 2024, Selangor's GDP reached its highest point in the ten-year period, showing strong post-pandemic growth. Other states/federal territories such as Kuala Lumpur and Johor also demonstrated steady increases over time, though at a smaller scale compared to Selangor. By 2024, overall GDP of Malaysia reached its highest level within the ten-year period.
